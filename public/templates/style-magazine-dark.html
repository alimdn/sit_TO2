FEATURE STORY

Designing for the Dark Side

Dark mode is more than just inverting colors. It's about understanding how light interacts with content, how contrast affects readability, and how atmosphere shapes the user experience. In this comprehensive guide, we explore the principles of dark design — from choosing the right grays to selecting accent colors that pop without overwhelming.

The best dark designs feel intentional, not just inverted. They use subtle gradients, careful spacing, and typography that breathes. The background isn't black — it's a deep charcoal that invites the eye to explore. Cards float with soft shadows. Text glows with just enough light to read comfortably.
